Questions, answered.

Is there a nutrition app that understands Indian food?

Yes — that's exactly why we built Svas. Most nutrition apps default to Western foods and leave you guessing at a katori of dal or a plate of pesarattu. Svas is built around regional Indian cuisine, so you can log the food you already eat and see real macros for it.

Which regional cuisines does Svas cover?

Svas spans dishes from 28 states — from Andhra pesarattu and Tamil ven pongal to Punjabi rajma chawal, Gujarati dhokla and Bengali macher jhol. Each dish carries its name in its own script, because your food deserves to be called by its real name.

Where does Svas get its nutrition data?

Every macro in Svas is computed from IFCT-2017 — the Indian Food Composition Tables published by the National Institute of Nutrition — and reviewed by dietitians. We don't scrape numbers off the internet or guess; if a value isn't verified, it doesn't ship.

Can Svas track macros and calories for home-cooked meals?

Yes. Svas knows the ingredients and portions behind everyday home cooking, so you get per-serving protein, carbs, fat and calories for the meals you actually make — not a generic "Indian curry" estimate.

Does Svas help with weight loss or muscle gain?

Svas builds a weekly plan around your goal — whether that's losing weight, gaining muscle, or simply eating healthier — using the regional food you already love. No imported meal plans, no foods you'd never cook.

How much does Svas cost?

Svas is free to know your food. Premium — which plans your whole week around it — is ₹99/month or ₹799/year, with a one-week free trial. Prices are in INR, taxes as applicable.
